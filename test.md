1. Horizontal Arpeggios (The Rendering Bug)
Look at measures 3, 6, and 11. The arpeggios (the wavy squiggly lines) are being drawn horizontally above and below the chords.

Why it's bad: In standard music notation, an arpeggio is a vertical wavy line placed directly to the left of a chord. Your synthetic dataset is teaching the model that arpeggios look like horizontal trills. When it sees a real, vertical arpeggio in the validation set, it won't recognize it.

The Fix: This happens because music21.expressions.ArpeggioMark() is sometimes treated as a horizontal spanner by default renderers if not explicitly anchored to the chord's geometry. You may need to use music21.articulations.ArpeggioMark() or force the rendering engine's layout settings to draw them vertically.

2. Broken Horizontal Spacing & Notehead Overlap
Look at the dense cluster of notes in measure 7. The noteheads are literally printing directly on top of each other, creating an illegible black blob.

Why it's bad: The network cannot learn the shape of a notehead or a stem if the pixels are mushed into a solid brick. It loses the visual boundaries of the objects.

The Fix: When you "shatter" a quarter note into four 16th notes in synthetic_generator.py, the measure is becoming mathematically overstuffed. You need to tell music21 to recalculate the measure layout, or your rendering engine (like Verovio) needs its layout parameters tweaked to force standard proportional spacing (e.g., setting a wider spacingLinear or spacingNonLinear value so the notes spread out).

3. Ambiguous Numbers (Tuplets vs. Fingerings)
Look at the numbers "3", "1", "2", and "5" scattered throughout (measures 2, 4, 7, 12).

Why it's bad: The model cannot visually differentiate a tupletNum from a fing (fingering) if there is no context. In measure 4, the "3" over the chord has no tuplet bracket. It looks exactly like a fingering number. The model is likely confusing the two classes constantly.

The Fix: You must force music21 to draw the brackets for tuplets. When you create the tuplet in synthetic_generator.py, add tup.bracket = True. This gives the model the visual context it needs to separate a triplet from a fingering mark.

4. "Flatlined" Slurs Slicing Through Stems
Look at the bottom staff, measure 12. The slur is drawn as a completely flat, straight line that slices directly horizontally through the stems of the beamed notes.

Why it's bad: While slurs do occasionally cross stems, rendering a perfectly straight line through them is an artifact of bad synthetic spacing. It breaks the visual continuity of the stems, which is likely why your stem mAP dropped.
